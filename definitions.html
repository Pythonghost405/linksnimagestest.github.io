Position Definitions

  • Chairman:The chairman is the highest-ranking officer in a company's board of directors. They are responsible for leading the board and ensuring that the company's goals and objectives are met. They also oversee the CEO and other senior executives and provide guidance and support to the company's management team.

  • President: The president is the highest-ranking officer in a company's management team. They are responsible for overseeing the day-to-day operations of the company and ensuring that the company's goals and objectives are met. They also work closely with the board of directors to develop and implement the company's strategic plans.

  • Managing Director:The managing director is responsible for managing the day-to-day operations of a company. They work closely with the CEO and other senior executives to develop and implement the company's strategic plans. They are also responsible for ensuring that the company's goals and objectives are met and that the company is operating efficiently and effectively.

  • Administrator: An administrator is responsible for managing the administrative functions of a company. They oversee the company's support staff, including receptionists, secretaries, and administrative assistants. They are responsible for ensuring that the company's administrative functions are running smoothly and efficiently.

  • Human Resource Manager:A human resource manager is responsible for managing the human resources functions of a company. They oversee the company's recruitment, training, and development programs, as well as its compensation and benefits programs. They are also responsible for ensuring that the company is in compliance with all applicable labor laws and regulations.
